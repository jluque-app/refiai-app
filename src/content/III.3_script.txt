I have mentioned before the concept “human capital”, what is it?
***CLICK***
It’s an intangible asset, its the economic value of a player’s experience and skills. 
Some individuals within the real estate industry have highly specialized skills and abilities in certain fields of the industry, whether it be development expertise, operational expertise, asset management expertise or capital management expertise. 
***CLICK***
All these players offer immense human capital, however if they do not have the capital to exploit that, then debt is the perfect opportunity to allow such industry experts to deploy their skills across a greater range of real estate assets.
***CLICK***
We must consider also that a real estate investor always tries to have a diversified portfolio, in other words, a large number of investments across a large range of different real estate asset types in order to reduce the risk. 
***CLICK***
Thanks to debt, an equity investor with limited capital is able to generate funds to increase the diversification of both the property types (residential, commercial, hotel etc.) and their geographic/market locations.
***CLICK***
To help clarify things, let’s see an example. 
***CLICK***
Suppose an equity investor with €10m worth of equity is only able to buy two properties worth €5m. 
***CLICK***
However, with a 75% LTV ratio debt, that same investor is able to diversify his investment over eight separate €5m properties. 
***CLICK***
Thanks to debt, he has increased the diversification of his portfolio and has reduced the risk.

Inflation is an important variable within the real estate market. 
***CLICK***
Property values and rent typically fluctuate according to inflation, however since expected inflation is built into the interest rate that the lender imposes on the debt, you cannot make money when borrowing during a period of high inflation – unless of course the lender charges you less than the market rate for your debt. 
In short, your real estate investment cannot benefit from inflation if it occurs before the start of the debt’s term. However, unexpected inflation during the debt term can be beneficial for real estate investments with fixed rate debt, because as inflation increases, the “real” cost of the debt decreases.
***CLICK***
To be clear, since it is a fixed rate debt, the debt service will remain the same (“fixed”) whereas the property’s cash flow will follow the same increasing trend as inflation, therefore improving the investment’s returns. Sometimes known as “reverse-inflation risk”, this can prove very beneficial to some investors.
