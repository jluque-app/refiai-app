Hello. In this video I will show you how to calculate an Adjustable Rate Mortgage.
***CLICK***
The first thing to do is to calculate the interest rate, which is done so by adding the market index with the margin: Interest Rate = Index + Margin
***CLICK***
The index tends to be LIBOR (London InterBank Offered Rate) or similar short-term rates, and as it fluctuates, so does the mortgage’s interest rate.
***CLICK***
In other words, ARMs’ interest rates are dependent on the financial market. 
***CLICK***
However, it is very important to remember, that several caps can restrict the increase of the interest rate. These are:
***CLICK***
The Annual Interest Rate Increase Cap
The Lifetime (of loan) Interest Rate Increase Cap and the 
Increase Payment Cap – the latter is not as common
***CLICK***
But, how often the interest rate is adjusted?
***CLICK***
Typically, the interest rate is adjusted in annual intervals, however they are sometimes adjusted more often.
***CLICK***
When the interest rate changes at the adjustment interval, so does the loan payment also changes:
***CLICK***
New payments are calculated based on:
The adjusted Interest Rate the current loan balance and the 
Remaining Amortization Period
